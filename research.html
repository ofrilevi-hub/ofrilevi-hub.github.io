Research

Research themes

We study how pathogenic fungi adapt to antifungal stress at the genetic, regulatory, and systems levels. Our work integrates functional genomics (CRISPRi), mechanistic microbiology, and multi-omics to identify pathways that govern resistance and tolerance, and to enable antifungal discovery, including plant-derived molecules.

1) Antifungal resistance and tolerance mechanisms

  • Genetic pathways that buffer antifungal stress and enable survival.
  • Membrane biology, stress signaling, and metabolic rewiring under drug pressure.
  • Quantitative phenotyping and synergy testing to map actionable combinations.

2) Genome-scale CRISPRi screens

  • Genome-wide CRISPRi perturbation to identify determinants of sensitivity and resistance.
  • Secondary validation, genetic interaction logic, and pathway-level interpretation.
  • Cross-model translation from yeast to pathogenic fungi when applicable.

3) Plant-derived antifungal compounds

  • Extract libraries and bioactivity-guided fractionation to pinpoint active molecules.
  • Mechanism-of-action discovery using genetics and multi-omics readouts.
  • Optimization of dosing, combinations, and functional validation in relevant models.

4) Integrative multi-omics

  • Transcriptomics and proteomics to characterize antifungal stress programs.
  • Network and enrichment analyses to connect genes, pathways, and phenotypes.
  • Hypothesis-driven validation using targeted genetics and mechanistic assays.
