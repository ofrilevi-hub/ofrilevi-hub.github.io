Research

Fungal resistance mechanisms and antifungal discovery

Our lab studies fungal resistance and tolerance, with an emphasis on how gene-expression regulation shapes antifungal stress responses in both clinically and agriculturally relevant contexts. In parallel, we investigate plant-derived antifungal molecules and leverage genome-scale CRISPRi screening to connect genes, pathways, and phenotypes.

By dissecting genetic and molecular programs that enable resistance and persistence, we aim to support the development of innovative, sustainable antifungal strategies.

Research areas

Antifungal resistance mechanisms

  • Genetic determinants and pathway logic that enable resistance and tolerance.
  • Stress signaling, membrane biology, and metabolic rewiring under drug pressure.
  • Actionable insights for antifungal strategies in agriculture and human health.

CRISPRi-based genomic screening

  • Unbiased, high-throughput CRISPRi screens to map sensitivity and resistance.
  • Secondary validation and mechanistic follow-up for top hits.
  • Pathway-level interpretation using integrative analysis.

Plant-derived antifungal compounds

  • Plant-extract libraries and bioactivity-guided fractionation to identify active molecules.
  • Mode-of-action discovery using genetics and multi-omics readouts.
  • Optimization of dosing and combinations, including synergy testing when relevant.

How we work

Functional genomics

Genome-scale perturbation (CRISPRi), targeted validation, and quantitative phenotyping to connect genotypes with antifungal-response phenotypes.

Integrative multi-omics

Transcriptomics and proteomics to characterize stress programs, supported by enrichment and network analyses to connect genes, pathways, and mechanisms.
