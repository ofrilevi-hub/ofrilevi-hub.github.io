Research

Fungal resistance mechanisms and antifungal discovery

Our lab studies fungal resistance and tolerance, with an emphasis on how gene-expression regulation shapes antifungal stress responses in clinically and agriculturally relevant contexts.

In parallel, we investigate plant-derived antifungal molecules and leverage genome-scale CRISPRi screening to connect genes, pathways, and phenotypes, supporting the development of innovative and sustainable antifungal strategies.

Current research projects

1

Genome-wide CRISPRi screening for antifungal resistance

We use genome-scale CRISPRi libraries to systematically perturb gene expression and map genetic determinants of sensitivity, resistance, and tolerance under antifungal stress.

  • Unbiased identification of pathways that shape antifungal response.
  • Prioritization of actionable targets for mechanistic follow-up.
  • Quantitative genotype-to-phenotype mapping under defined drug or compound exposures.
2

Plant-derived antifungal discovery and quantitative phenotyping

We screen plant-derived extracts and purified molecules, then quantify growth inhibition and recovery dynamics using kinetic plate-reader assays to capture resistance and tolerance phenotypes.

  • Bioactivity-guided fractionation to identify active antifungal molecules.
  • Growth-curve based metrics to separate inhibition from tolerance and persistence.
  • Combination testing to identify synergistic interactions with existing antifungals.
3

Mechanistic dissection using integrative multi-omics

We integrate transcriptomics, proteomics, and targeted validation to connect molecular programs to antifungal phenotypes and define mechanism-of-action hypotheses grounded in reproducible data.

  • Pathway-level interpretation of CRISPRi hits and compound responses.
  • Orthogonal validation using genetics, microscopy, and functional assays.
  • Cross-condition comparison to identify conserved versus context-specific response modules.

Typical outputs

Ranked genetic determinants, pathway enrichments, interaction networks, and validated mechanistic models of antifungal response.

Research areas

Antifungal resistance mechanisms

Genetic determinants and pathway logic that enable resistance and tolerance, including stress signaling, membrane biology, and metabolic rewiring under drug pressure.

CRISPRi-based genomic screening

Genome-wide functional genomics to map sensitivity and resistance, followed by targeted validation and mechanistic dissection of key hits.

Plant-derived antifungal compounds

Extraction, fractionation, and identification of antifungal molecules, including testing against pathogenic fungi and exploration of drug synergy.

Integrative multi-omics

Transcriptomics and proteomics integration to define fungal stress programs and link molecular changes to phenotypic outcomes.
