Research

Fungal resistance mechanisms and antifungal discovery

The Levi Lab studies how pathogenic fungi adapt to antifungal stress through genetic, regulatory, and systems-level mechanisms. We integrate genome-scale CRISPRi screening with mechanistic microbiology and multi-omics to identify pathways that govern resistance, tolerance, and persistence. In parallel, we investigate plant-derived antifungal molecules as sources for novel antifungal interventions.

Current research directions

Genome-wide CRISPRi screening for antifungal resistance

Systematic perturbation of gene expression using pooled CRISPR interference libraries enables high-resolution mapping of resistance and sensitivity determinants. Hits are prioritized for mechanistic validation using targeted approaches.

  • Genotype to phenotype mapping under antifungal stress
  • Identification of genetic buffering pathways
  • Integration with proteomics and transcriptomics

Plant-derived antifungal compounds

Extracts and purified plant molecules are screened for antifungal activity and phenotypic outcomes are quantified using kinetic growth assays. Active fractions undergo structural analysis and follow-up testing for synergistic interactions with existing drugs.

  • Bioactivity guided fractionation
  • Quantitative growth curve analysis and modeling
  • Compound synergy testing with clinical antifungals

Integrative multi-omics and mechanistic validation

Multi-omics datasets guide mechanistic dissection of antifungal response programs. Candidate determinants of resistance are validated through genetics, microscopy, and biochemical approaches to define testable models of antifungal mode of action.

  • Transcriptomics and proteomics integration
  • Targeted genetic validation using CRISPR based tools
  • Structure function approaches for candidate pathways

Research areas

Antifungal resistance mechanisms

Pathways and stress programs that drive resistance and tolerance under drug pressure.

Functional genomics using CRISPRi

High resolution functional mapping of genetic determinants of phenotype.

Plant derived antifungal discovery

Extraction, fractionation, and testing of plant compounds against fungal pathogens.

Multi omics integration

Proteomics and transcriptomics to define mechanistic hypotheses and response networks.
