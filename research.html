Research

Fungal resistance mechanisms and antifungal discovery

The Levi Lab studies fungal resistance and tolerance, with a focus on how gene-expression regulation shapes antifungal stress responses in clinically and agriculturally relevant contexts.

We combine genome-scale CRISPRi screening, mechanistic microbiology, plant-derived antifungal discovery, and integrative multi-omics approaches to connect genes, pathways, and phenotypes.

MIGAL research profile

Genome-scale CRISPRi screening for antifungal resistance

We employ genome-wide CRISPRi libraries to systematically perturb gene expression and identify genetic determinants of antifungal sensitivity, resistance, and tolerance.

Competitive growth assays under antifungal stress are combined with high-throughput sequencing and enrichment analysis to map resistance-associated pathways and processes across the fungal genome.

Plant-derived antifungal compounds and phenotypic profiling

We screen diverse libraries of plant-derived compounds to identify antifungal activities against pathogenic fungi.

Growth inhibition, dose-response behavior, and drug interactions are quantified using plate-reader based assays, enabling systematic phenotypic characterization and synergy analysis with existing antifungal drugs.

Integrative multi-omics and mechanistic insight

Transcriptomic and proteomic profiling are integrated with CRISPRi screening results to define fungal stress-response programs and identify convergent resistance mechanisms.

This systems-level approach enables mechanistic interpretation of antifungal responses and supports the rational development of novel, sustainable antifungal strategies.
