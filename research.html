Research

Fungal resistance mechanisms and antifungal discovery

Our lab studies fungal resistance and tolerance, with an emphasis on how gene-expression regulation shapes antifungal stress responses in both clinically and agriculturally relevant contexts.

In parallel, we investigate plant-derived antifungal molecules and leverage genome-scale CRISPRi screening to connect genes, pathways, and phenotypes, aiming to support the development of innovative and sustainable antifungal strategies.

Research areas

Antifungal resistance mechanisms

We investigate genetic determinants and pathway logic that enable resistance and tolerance, focusing on stress signaling, membrane biology, and metabolic rewiring under antifungal pressure.

CRISPRi-based genomic screening

Genome-wide CRISPRi screens are used to map sensitivity and resistance, followed by targeted validation and mechanistic dissection of key hits.

Plant-derived antifungal compounds

We apply bioactivity-guided fractionation of plant extracts to identify antifungal molecules and characterize their modes of action.

Integrative multi-omics

Transcriptomics and proteomics are integrated to define fungal stress programs and link molecular changes to phenotypic outcomes.

Experimental framework

We employ pooled CRISPRi libraries to systematically perturb gene expression under antifungal stress, followed by sequencing-based quantification and pathway-level analysis.

Growth kinetics and dose-response assays are used to characterize antifungal activity, resistance, and potential synergistic interactions with existing drugs.
