Research

Fungal resistance mechanisms and antifungal discovery

Our research focuses on understanding how pathogenic fungi adapt to antifungal stress at genetic, regulatory, and systems levels, across both clinical and agricultural contexts.

We integrate genome-scale CRISPRi screening, mechanistic microbiology, and multi-omics approaches to identify pathways governing resistance and tolerance, and to support the development of innovative antifungal strategies.

Research areas

Antifungal resistance mechanisms

Genetic determinants, stress signaling pathways, membrane biology, and metabolic rewiring that enable fungal resistance and tolerance under antifungal pressure.

CRISPRi-based genomic screening

Genome-wide CRISPRi screens to map sensitivity and resistance, followed by targeted validation and pathway-level interpretation.

Plant-derived antifungal compounds

Identification and characterization of antifungal molecules from plant extracts, including bioactivity-guided fractionation and mode-of-action discovery.

Integrative multi-omics

Transcriptomic and proteomic profiling to dissect fungal stress programs and connect molecular changes to phenotypic outcomes.

Genome-scale CRISPRi screening

We employ genome-wide CRISPRi libraries to systematically perturb gene expression and quantify fitness effects under antifungal stress. This approach enables unbiased identification of genes and pathways governing resistance and tolerance.

Quantitative phenotyping

High-resolution growth measurements and fitness profiling are used to characterize fungal responses to antifungal compounds, enabling robust comparison across genetic and chemical perturbations.
