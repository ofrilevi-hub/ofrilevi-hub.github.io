Levi Lab

Fungal resistance mechanisms and antifungal discovery

We study how pathogenic fungi adapt to antifungal stress in clinically and agriculturally relevant settings. Our work integrates genome-scale CRISPRi screening, plant-derived antifungal discovery, and quantitative phenotyping to identify molecular targets and resistance pathways.

Research pillars

Genome-scale CRISPRi screens

Functional genomics in yeast and pathogenic fungi to map gene networks that mediate drug response, resistance, and tolerance.

Read more

Plant-derived antifungal discovery

High-throughput screening and bioactivity-guided fractionation to identify active molecules from plant extracts, followed by rigorous validation in relevant fungal systems.

Read more

Quantitative phenotyping

Growth kinetics, MIC, tolerance metrics, and combination studies to connect molecular mechanisms to robust, reproducible antifungal phenotypes.
